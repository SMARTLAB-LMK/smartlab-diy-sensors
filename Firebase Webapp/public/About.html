Can New Thinking Help Reduce Your Energy Bills? – SMARTLAB Project Launches at the Citizen Innovation Lab in Limerick

Opportunity for Building Owners & Occupants to Install Free Sensors to Monitor and Reduce Building Energy Use     

Limerick, Ireland: An exciting new research project in the city is testing new ways to make Limerick buildings smart-ready for a clean energy future. Smarter buildings can respond to the needs of their occupants, cost less to run, and are ready to connect to an energy grid powered by solar and wind power. 

The SEAI-funded SMARTLAB project from the University of Limerick is inviting local building owners and occupants to receive free energy monitors and environmental sensors. SMARTLAB will install these sensors in local buildings, provide free expert analysis of how healthy and energy efficient their building currently is, and give advice on what can be done to make buildings both cheaper and greener

“This is a cutting-edge project that places Limerick at the forefront of collaborative approaches to empowering smart energy citizens. We’re looking forward to working with people in Limerick city centre to explore how they can use smart sensor technology in their buildings and be part of the clean energy transition. The knowledge we develop together will be shared widely with policymakers at national and international levels.” Prof. Stephen Kinsella, SMARTLAB’s Principal Investigator and Head of the Department of Economics at UL

Dr. Ruth Kerrigan, Head of R&D for project partner Integrated Environmental Solutions, says “SMARTLAB will give people a chance to see how their buildings can work more effectively on their behalf. We’ll provide private dashboards for each participant to access expert analysis of their building’s data, and we expect to see a 10% reduction in energy use through the project.”

Head of Climate Action and Innovation at Limerick City and County Council, Rosie Webb also says, “given the energy crisis and the cost of energy retrofit, this is an important opportunity for people in Limerick to explore how they can reduce their energy use immediately by using sensor data to show where the opportunities lie.”   The project was launched at the Citizen Innovation Lab, a collaborative space hosted by Limerick City and County Council and the University of Limerick. The Lab space with Fab Lab Limerick will be open to project participants for learning events, drop-ins, and sensor demonstrations. To learn more about the project and apply to get your own sensor installation, see smartlablimerick.ie

The SMARTLAB project, based at Limerick’s Citizen Innovation Lab, is funded by the SEAI Research Development and Demonstration (RD&D) Funding Programme. Limerick City and County Council, Integrated Environmental Solutions, The Convex Lens, and Carrig Conservation International are all project partners and the project is led by University of Limerick.

This project has been funded by the Sustainable Energy Authority Ireland under the SEAI National Energy Research, Development and Demonstration Funding Programme 2021, Grant number 21/RDD/634.
